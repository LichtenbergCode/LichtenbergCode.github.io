# Non Technical Interview

## Mindset 
Your Life is not over if you do not pass the interview
You can continue searching for more oportunities in other companies. <br>
Not expecting the Job, Do your best but you don't expect something. <br>
**Do Less**: The less you do, the more you do. <br>

*This is not your only chance*

* The Interviewer is a person too, Be excited and try to transmit good energy, remember your interviewer is like you, just a person, trying to do his job. 

## What do you have to demostrate? 
* You have to answer the next three questions (behavior question): 
    * Can you do the Job? 
    * Can I work with you? 
    * Are you going to improve? 

* 4 Heroes (Prepare 4 histories to present yourself)
    * Technical Hero (Are you a technical person?)
    * Success Hero (Are you a succesfull person?)
    * Leadership Hero (Do you have Leadership qualities?)
    * Challenge Hero (How do you face to something?)
    * This 4 Heroes will answer the three fundamental questions that you have to demostrate to the interviewer. 

## Tell me about yourself?
You have a limit time to talk about you and convence to the interviewer that you are a good candidate Focus the conversation in demostrate that you are a good candidate for the position.

Talk about How everything that you have done in the past make you perfect to get the position. 

Demostrate that each day you become better and better

* your triggers of success
* Mention things you want to get asked
* Skills should be relevant to job


## Why us? 
Why do you want to work for us? 
* Make the interviewer feel special.
* Make to feel to the interviewer that you really want that company, that you know that company.
* You have to investigate about the company. 
---
* Show you want to grow
* Demonstrate why you are the best for the job

Why did you leave your Job? 
* Try to be as positive as you possible, try to not to be negative

## Tell me about a problem that you have solved 
Tell me about problem + how you solved it 
* Have this prepared 
* Have metrics and numbers
* Scaling, performance, security

For this kind of question fallow the **SAR** method. <br>
S: Situation <br>
A: Action <br>
R: Resolve <br>

Be specific,You don't want to sound like a pedant but you do not have to leave the oportunity to talk good about you, a good way to talk about you without sound like a pedant is being specific in the problems that you have solved. <br>
**It is not the same to say**: "I improve the performance of a website." <br>
**To say**: "The website performance improved by 50%" <br>

The first one sounds ambiguous and not clear enough while the second one sounds like a fact, it's more difficult to constraint something that was measure, and it's more difficult to believe that is a lie.

Focus on big projects, You cannot really talk so much about small projects, it does not sound impressive. 

*People don't really want to work with complainers. They want to work with people that just get to work, solve problems, and move on.*

## What is your biggest weakness?
Don't say something like: 
* I work to hard 
* I care too much about my coworkers

*If you say that you'll sound like a liar and you don't want that*

Remember, you want that the interviewer thinks that you are the best option, that you are the person that he wants to work with. 

You Say: 
* Real answer 
* Show how you improved it

## Any questions for me? 
* Focus on them, not company (YOU)
* Mention something they mentioned

**Ask something like:** <br>
1. What is the greatest mistake you made in the first month of the job and what did you do? 
2. Where do you see this company going in the next three years?
3. Why did you join this company? 
4. have you seen your skills grow in the past year? 
5. What they wish somebody told them when they just started the job of this company?
